What is Fluid Art or Paint Pouring?

Fluid art, also known as flow art, is a form of abstract art that involves pouring, dripping, or otherwise manipulating various types of liquid paints or pigments onto a surface to create unique, colorful compositions. The process typically involves combining different colors of paint, adding various additives like silicone oil, and then pouring or tilting the canvas or other surface to create fluid, organic patterns and designs.


The resulting artwork is often characterized by its vibrant colors, swirling shapes, and unpredictable, one-of-a-kind patterns. Fluid art is a popular form of contemporary art and can be created using a variety of different techniques and materials, including acrylic paint, resin, and alcohol inks.
